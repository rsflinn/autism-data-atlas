Too Many Connections

As children grow, the brain removes connections that aren't being used -- like weeding a garden so the important plants get more sunlight. In autistic brains, this "pruning" happens less. The result is a denser tangle of wiring -- more noise, more competing signals, and a harder time picking out what matters.
