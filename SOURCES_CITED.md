## MSSNG Dataset Sources

## SSC Dataset Sources

### Cohort Overview and Phenotyping
- https://www.sfari.org/resource/simons-simplex-collection/
  - SFARI SSC resource page
  - Source for: ~2,600 families; 80% with unaffected sibling; access information

- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20955926/ (PMID: 20955926; Fischbach & Lord 2010)
  - Neuron. 2010;68(2):192-195. DOI: 10.1016/j.neuron.2010.05.014
  - Source for: Original SSC resource description; simplex family design rationale; deep phenotyping protocol

### WGS Data Availability
- https://cohorts-cdn.simonsfoundation.org/SSC/release_notes/DS0000101/SSC-WGS-CSHL-genotypes-2025-06.pdf
  - SSC WGS release notes
  - Source for: 6,383 individuals from 2,274 families with WGS data; SFARI_SSC_WGS_2a release information

### Landmark De Novo Variant Discovery Papers
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25363768/ (PMID: 25363768; Iossifov et al. 2014)
  - Nature. 2014;515(7526):216-221. DOI: 10.1038/nature13908
  - Source for: Exome sequencing of >2,500 SSC families; 30% of cases driven by de novo mutations; sex differences (45% in females); genome-wide de novo discovery

- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26364307/ (PMID: 26364307; Sanders et al. 2015)
  - Neuron. 2015;88(6):1226-1239. DOI: 10.1016/j.neuron.2015.09.029
  - Source for: TADA analysis combining SSC and ASC; 71 high-confidence autism risk loci; definitive gene list

- https://www.nature.com/articles/nrg3585
  - Source for: General background on de novo mutations in autism genetics

### Noncoding Variant Analysis
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40468385/
  - 2024 publication on de novo noncoding variants with autism
  - Source for: De novo noncoding variants contribute to 4.3% of autism cases; integration with gene expression and sex information

### Comparative Analysis
- https://www.nature.com/articles/s42003-021-02533-z
  - Source for: Rates of contributory de novo mutations in high and low-risk families
