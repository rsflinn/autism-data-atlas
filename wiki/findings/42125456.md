# Genetic risk for neurodevelopmental disorders as a potential factor affecting antipsychotic responsiveness in schizophrenia: a postmortem brain study

## Key Findings

In postmortem brains from 24 schizophrenia patients and 48 controls, ADHD-PRS and ASD-PRS were tested against antipsychotic responsiveness scores. The antipsychotic responsiveness score for positive symptoms showed a suggestive negative correlation with ADHD-PRS and a positive correlation with ASD-PRS, but neither survived multiple-testing correction. Differential expression analysis identified 1,773 DEGs enriched in neuronal system and mitochondrial pathways.

## Relevance to Atlas

Underpowered (n=19 with clinical data) and the headline correlations are not significant after correction. Reportable as exploratory only. The use of ASD-PRS derived from common variants on a small postmortem cohort is methodologically relevant to the broader question of how ASD common-variant load expresses across psychiatric phenotypes.

None named in abstract.

## Methods

GWAS data from postmortem brains, ADHD-PRS and ASD-PRS from public GWAS, differential expression and pathway analysis in prefrontal cortex.
