# Somatic variants activating the RAS-MAPK pathway confer susceptibility to hippocampal sclerosis in drug-resistant epilepsy

## Key Findings
Hippocampal sclerosis in pediatric epilepsy surgery has been regarded as acquired but commonly co-occurs with focal cortical dysplasia (FCD IIIa). Somatic variants in the RAS-MAPK pathway identified as conferring susceptibility. Reframes hippocampal sclerosis as having a genetic (somatic) component.

## Relevance to Atlas
Relevant to drug-resistant epilepsy genetics (Teagan's comorbidity). Somatic variant mechanism is distinct from germline but demonstrates how mosaic genetic variation can produce focal brain pathology and seizures. RAS-MAPK is upstream of ERK signaling, which connects to SYNGAP1 biology.
