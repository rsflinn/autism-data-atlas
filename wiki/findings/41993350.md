# Instability of Alpha Oscillatory States in Autism and Familial Liability: Evidence from Burst-Resolved High-Density EEG

## Key Findings
High-density EEG study of 117 children (52 autistic, 26 siblings, 39 non-autistic), ages 8-14. Autistic children showed shorter alpha burst duration and reduced alpha abundance over parietal regions, with siblings at intermediate values (familial liability gradient). Single-event analyses showed burst amplitude did not differ between groups; alpha power reductions were driven by reduced temporal stability rather than weaker oscillations.

## Relevance to Atlas
Endophenotype study, not genetics, but the sibling-intermediate pattern is consistent with polygenic liability and quantifies an autism endophenotype potentially usable in future GWAS or variant-phenotype mapping.

None in atlas 49-gene set

## Methods
High-density EEG, periodic/aperiodic parameter separation (specparam), burst-resolved single-event analysis, ordinal sibling comparisons.
