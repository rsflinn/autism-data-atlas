# Linking Genetic Risk to Disease-Relevant Cellular States via Metacell-Informed Modeling with ICePop

## Key Findings
ICePop performs GWAS-to-cell-type association at metacell resolution, balancing statistical power with ability to detect heterogeneous disease signals within annotated cell types. Applied across 81 traits and 120 cell types. In ASD, identifies preferential enrichment of genetic risk in specific enteric neuron subtypes, implicating enteric nervous system dysfunction in GI comorbidities.

## Relevance to Atlas
Provides a new computational framework for mapping GWAS signals to specific cellular states within broader cell types. The ASD-enteric neuron finding connects genetic risk to GI comorbidities through a specific cell population, relevant to understanding phenotypic heterogeneity.

## Genes
No specific atlas genes highlighted; uses genome-wide ASD GWAS signals.

## Methods
Metacell aggregation, disease-cell type association testing, comparison with seismic and scDRS, Tabula Muris dataset, disease clustering by cellular association profiles.
