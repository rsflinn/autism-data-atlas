# In vivo human embryonic spinal cord atlas validates stem cell-derived human dorsal interneurons and reveals ASD spinal signatures

## Key Findings
Integrated six scRNA-seq datasets from human embryonic spinal cord (GW 4-25) to create reference atlas of somatosensory circuit development. Identified molecular signatures of dI4 and dI5 interneuron expansion/specialization (mechanosensory and nociceptive processing). ASD-associated genes enriched within mechanosensory interneuron populations.

## Relevance to Atlas
Extends ASD genetic risk beyond brain to spinal cord somatosensory circuits. The finding that ASD genes are enriched in mechanosensory interneurons connects to sensory processing differences observed clinically in autism. Provides a new tissue context for understanding ASD gene function.

ASD gene set enrichment (specific genes not listed in abstract)

## Methods
Integration of 6 scRNA-seq datasets, neuromesodermal progenitor differentiation, in vivo vs. in vitro comparison, gene set enrichment analysis.
