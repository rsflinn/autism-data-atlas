# SPG601 BK channel activator in Fragile X syndrome

## Key Findings
First clinical evidence that a BK (big potassium) channel activator produces significant neurophysiological changes in FXS. In 10 adult men with FXS, single-dose SPG601 800mg reduced excessive gamma power (p=0.023), modulated alpha2 (p<0.001) and theta (p=0.008) bands, and improved executive function on the NIH Toolbox Flanker test (p=0.027) vs placebo.

## Relevance to Atlas
Directly tests the ion channel dysfunction hypothesis downstream of FMRP loss. BK channels are potassium channels whose dysfunction is a predicted consequence of FMRP loss -- this trial provides clinical proof-of-concept that correcting specific ion channel deficits can normalize brain activity patterns in a monogenic autism-related condition.

## Genes
No specific atlas genes mentioned, but mechanism involves BK (KCNMA1) channels downstream of FMRP

## Methods
Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, 2-period balanced crossover study (n=10). Resting-state EEG, auditory-evoked gamma, NIH Toolbox cognitive assessments, CGI scales.
