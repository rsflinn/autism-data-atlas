# Federated single-cell QTL meta-analysis reveals novel disease mechanisms

## Key Findings
Federated single-cell cis-eQTL meta-analysis across 12 PBMC datasets (2,032 individuals, 2.5M cells) identified eQTLs for 6,592 genes across 6 immune cell types. 42% of eQTLs undetected in bulk showed STRONGER disease GWAS enrichment. Integrated cis- and trans-eQTLs to reconstruct 6,382 trans-eGenes (37.2% novel) with upstream regulators in directed regulatory relationships.

## Relevance to Atlas
Methodologically critical for the atlas. Demonstrates that cell-type-specific eQTLs invisible in bulk tissue are MORE enriched for disease GWAS loci -- directly supporting the hypothesis that noncoding regulatory variants act through cell-type-specific mechanisms. Although this study used immune cells, the principle applies to brain cell types. The approach of reconstructing directed regulatory networks from cis-to-trans-eQTL integration is relevant to the atlas's goal of mapping regulatory variant effects.
