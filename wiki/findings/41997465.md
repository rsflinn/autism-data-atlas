# Increasing MeCP2 protein in Pitt-Hopkins syndrome model (Tcf4) mice does not affect abnormal myelination but induces the generation of astrocytes

## Key Findings
Increasing MeCP2 levels in Tcf4-heterozygous mice (PTHS model) produces behavioral rescue that extends to contextual fear learning but does not correct the oligodendrocyte maturation arrest or dramatically alter the Tcf4-heterozygous transcriptome. MECP2 transgene expression in Tcf4 mice instead increases Olig2/Gfap co-expressing cells and astrocytic-morphology cells, pointing to MeCP2-TCF4 interplay in astrocyte development rather than myelination correction.

## Relevance to Atlas
Direct functional data on two core NDD genes in the atlas gene set, TCF4 and MECP2. Disambiguates which downstream processes are or are not coupled to behavioral rescue: myelination is not the mechanism, astrocyte generation is a candidate.

## Genes
TCF4, MECP2

## Methods
Tcf4 heterozygous and knock-in mouse lines crossed with MECP2 transgene; RNA-sequencing; protein expression (Olig2, Gfap); behavioral assays including contextual fear learning.
