# NeuroPathX: Explainable Imaging-Genetics Associations in Neurological Disorders

## Key Findings
NeuroPathX is an explainable deep learning framework that uses cross-attention mechanisms for early fusion of MRI brain structural data and genetic pathway data. A sparsity loss focuses on salient interactions while a pathway similarity loss enforces consistent representations. Validated on both ASD and Alzheimer's disease cohorts, outperforming baselines and revealing biologically plausible brain-genetics associations.

## Relevance to Atlas
Methods paper with ASD validation. The pathway-level genetic integration approach could be applied to test whether regulatory variant burden in specific gene sets associates with brain structural variation in ASD. Not directly about regulatory variants, but the framework is relevant to connecting genetic architecture to phenotypic variation.
