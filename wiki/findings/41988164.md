# MAGEB16 as an epigenetic timing regulator linking X-chromosome biology to neurodevelopmental vulnerability in Autism Spectrum Disorder

## Key Findings
Integrative review positioning MAGEB16 (X-linked cancer-testis antigen) as an epigenetically regulated modifier of neurodevelopment. Combines depletion data from pluripotent stem cells, perinatal cord-blood methylome data from ASD cohorts and peripheral transcriptomics. Authors propose MAGEB16 acts as a dosage- and timing-dependent regulator of early lineage commitment, where disruption of its promoter DNA-methylation switch during X-chromosome-enriched developmental windows influences NDD risk.

## Relevance to Atlas
Connects promoter methylation regulation to autism vulnerability via an X-linked gene not currently in the atlas gene set, but methodologically relevant for the project's interest in noncoding regulatory variation and epigenetic timing. Hypothesis-generating; not a primary data paper.
