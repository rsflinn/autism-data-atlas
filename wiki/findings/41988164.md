# MAGEB16 as an epigenetic timing regulator linking X-chromosome biology to neurodevelopmental vulnerability in ASD

## Key Findings
MAGEB16 is an X-linked cancer-testis antigen whose expression is controlled by a promoter DNA-methylation switch. Integrative analysis of chromosomal context, interaction networks, cord-blood methylome data from ASD cohorts, and stem cell depletion datasets positions MAGEB16 as a dosage- and timing-dependent regulator of early lineage commitment. Disruption of its epigenetic control during X-chromosome-enriched developmental periods may influence neurodevelopmental pathways toward ASD phenotypes.

## Relevance to Atlas
Directly relevant to the noncoding/epigenetic regulatory variant framework. The methylation-dependent promoter switch mechanism is exactly the type of regulatory architecture that could harbor common noncoding variants affecting autism risk. The X-linkage also connects to the male bias in autism prevalence. However, this is a review/synthesis paper proposing a model rather than presenting primary experimental data, so evidence tier is C (descriptive).

## Genes

## Methods
