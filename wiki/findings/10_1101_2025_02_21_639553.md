# FMRP regulates neuronal RNA granules, not ribosome stalling sites

## Key Findings
Loss of FMRP does not change where ribosomes stall on mRNAs or the identity of stalled-ribosome-associated proteins. However, FMRP loss reduces the number of neuronal RNA granules containing stalled ribosomes, and the remaining granules are resistant to reactivation. This clarifies FMRP's role: it regulates the granule structures that house stalled ribosomes, not the stalling process itself.

## Relevance to Atlas
Refines the molecular mechanism downstream of FMRP loss (the primary genetic cause of autism-associated Fragile X syndrome). Since ion channel mRNAs are among those regulated by FMRP-associated stalled ribosomes, this connects to the ion channel convergence pathway. The granule-level (not transcript-level) regulation suggests a structural/organizational role for FMRP.
