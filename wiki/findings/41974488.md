# The forkhead transcription factor FKH-7/FOXP acts in C. elegans chemosensory neurons to shape a life history strategy

## Key Findings
Human FOXP1 functionally substitutes for C. elegans FKH-7 in chemosensory neurons controlling a developmental decision (diapause entry). A hypomorphic missense mutation associated with human NDD characterized by social interaction deficits reduces the frequency of the adaptive response. Single-cell transcriptomics of chemosensory neurons in the FOXP1 missense variant identified candidate genes for variant-specific molecular pathology at single-neuron resolution.

## Relevance to Atlas
FOXP1 is one of the core upstream transcription factors in the atlas gene set. This paper provides direct functional evidence that a human NDD-associated FOXP1 variant alters neuronal decision-making in a conserved sensory circuit, and identifies downstream transcriptomic consequences at single-cell resolution. This is rare -- most FOXP1 functional work is in mouse; this offers single-neuron-level mechanistic detail.

FOXP1

## Methods
C. elegans genetics (FKH-7 mutants and humanized alleles); single-cell transcriptomics of chemosensory neurons; behavioral assays for dauer entry; cross-species complementation.
