# The forkhead transcription factor FKH-7/FOXP acts in C. elegans chemosensory neurons to shape a life history strategy

## Key Findings
Human FOXP1 functionally substitutes for C. elegans FKH-7 in chemosensory neurons controlling adaptive developmental decisions. An NDD-associated hypomorphic missense mutation reduces the frequency of adaptive response in worm populations. Single-cell transcriptomics of variant-expressing neurons identifies candidate genes for variant-specific molecular pathology at single-neuron resolution.

## Relevance to Atlas
Direct functional evidence for FOXP1 -- an atlas transcription factor -- in sensory neuron function. Demonstrates cross-species conservation of FOXP1 function and provides single-cell molecular readouts of an NDD-associated variant. Connects FOXP1 to sensory processing and adaptive behavior, relevant to autism phenotype.

## Genes
Atlas genes: FOXP1

## Methods
C. elegans genetics; human FOXP1 rescue experiments; single-cell RNA-seq of chemosensory neurons; behavioral assays of dauer formation.
