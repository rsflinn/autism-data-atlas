# Genetic spectrum and risk factor analysis for drug-resistant early-onset epilepsy

## Key Findings
In 75 infants with early-onset epilepsy, drug-resistant epilepsy (DRE) had significantly higher rates of definitive genetic diagnosis (71.4% vs 37.5%, p=0.003). KCNQ2 and SCN2A were recurrent variants in the DRE group. Abnormal muscle tone (68.6% vs 25.0%), feeding difficulties (37.1% vs 12.5%), and epileptiform EEG discharges (82.9% vs 30.0%) were all significantly associated with drug resistance. Developmental delay was far more prevalent in DRE (77.4% vs 25.7%).

## Relevance to Atlas
Connects two atlas ion channel genes (KCNQ2, SCN2A) directly to drug-resistant epilepsy phenotype. Supports the atlas finding that ion channel disruption is a final common pathway in NDD gene networks. Relevant to severity-genotype questions and autism+epilepsy comorbidity (Teagan has drug-resistant epilepsy).

## Genes
Atlas genes: KCNQ2, SCN2A

## Methods
Retrospective cohort of 75 infants at a tertiary neonatal center (2018-2025). Next-generation sequencing. Clinical phenotyping including EEG, MRI, developmental assessment. Univariate risk factor analysis.
