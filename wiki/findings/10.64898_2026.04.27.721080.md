# Connecting polygenic disease risk to cell states and regulatory programs through single-cell chromatin accessibility (SCADS)

## Key Findings

SCADS is a new computational framework that links GWAS summary statistics to scATAC-seq data to score individual cells by disease relevance. It uses topic modeling to find chromatin co-regulatory regions, quantifies their polygenic GWAS enrichment, then computes per-cell disease scores. In simulations it outperforms existing methods on power while controlling false positives. Applied to autoimmune traits, it shows substantial within-cell-type heterogeneity in disease relevance and pinpoints specific gene programs (CD8+ T cells and colon epithelial cells in IBD).

## Relevance to Atlas

Methodologically directly applicable to the atlas's noncoding-variant workstream. The atlas has been pursuing GWAS-to-cell-state linkage for autism noncoding loci, and SCADS provides a calibrated, cell-level scoring framework that could be applied to the Grove 2019 ASD GWAS against published fetal/adult brain scATAC-seq atlases. The topic-modeling step for chromatin co-regulation is a more flexible alternative to the per-peak ChromBPNet approach already tested. Worth evaluating as a complement to (or replacement for) population-burden scoring on autism GWAS signals.

## Genes

None directly. Framework-level paper applicable to all 49 atlas genes via downstream cell-type prioritization.

## Methods

Topic modeling on scATAC-seq peak-by-cell matrix to identify chromatin co-regulatory programs. GWAS summary stats overlaid via stratified LD score regression-style enrichment. Per-cell scores aggregated from topic weights times enrichment levels. Open-source method (CC-BY license, code presumably available with paper).
