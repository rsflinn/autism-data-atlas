# A single-cell multiomic analysis identifies molecular and gene-regulatory mechanisms dysregulated in developing Down syndrome neocortex

## Key Findings

Mid-gestation snMultiomics of 26 human donors finds DS neocortex has reduced progenitors, accelerated neuronal specification, and GWAS signal enrichment in DS-altered chromatin. Explicitly identifies cell-specific regulatory pathways shared across neurodevelopmental disorders.

## Relevance to Atlas

Adds a large mid-gestation human snMultiomics dataset that can cross-reference regulatory changes in 49-gene-set loci. The paper explicitly identifies GRNs and GWAS enrichment in DS-altered chromatin and shared NDD pathways, making it a candidate for cross-condition regulatory comparison with autism.

## Methods

snMultiomics (snRNA + snATAC) on 26 donor mid-gestation neocortex samples; cell interaction network inference; GRN inference; GWAS enrichment in DS-altered chromatin
