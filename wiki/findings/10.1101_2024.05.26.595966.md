# Complementary vertebrate Wac models exhibit phenotypes relevant to DeSanto-Shinawi Syndrome

## Key Findings
Mouse and zebrafish Wac deletion mutants recapitulate DeSanto-Shinawi Syndrome phenotypes: craniofacial abnormalities, behavioral changes, seizure susceptibility, and sex-different brain volume changes. Both models showed impacts to GABAergic neurons. Transcriptomic analysis of Wac loss-of-function in mice reveals downstream molecular effects.

## Relevance to Atlas
WAC is not in the current 49-gene atlas set, but DeSanto-Shinawi Syndrome includes autism and seizures as core phenotypes -- the same comorbidity pattern we study. The GABAergic neuron involvement and seizure susceptibility connect to the ion channel/inhibitory neuron themes in the atlas. The transcriptomic data from Wac KO could be compared with other NDD gene perturbation datasets (Lipton, Paulsen CHOOSE).

WAC (directly studied; not currently in atlas gene set but phenotypically relevant).

## Methods
Mouse and zebrafish Wac knockout models, behavioral phenotyping, seizure susceptibility testing, brain volumetric MRI, transcriptomics.
