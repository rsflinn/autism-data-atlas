# Genetic Origin of Uneven Cognitive Profiles in Heritable Neurodevelopmental Conditions

## Key Findings
Computational simulations combining genetic algorithms and artificial neural networks demonstrate how polygenic effects on shared neurocomputational substrate properties can produce uneven cognitive profiles across domains. The "domain-relevance" model shows that selection tuning substrate properties for one cognitive function can enhance or impair other functions depending on computational demands. Behavioral deficits associate with increased heritability of individual differences.

## Relevance to Atlas
Theoretically relevant to understanding how polygenic common variant architecture (which accounts for ~49% of autism variance) produces variable phenotypic profiles. The model's prediction that behavioral deficits associate with increased heritability aligns with observations about autism's polygenic architecture. However, this is purely computational with no real genetic data.

## Genes
No specific genes; operates at the level of polygenic architecture theory.

## Methods
Genetic algorithms + artificial neural networks, 20-generation selection simulations, 5-task domain model on shared substrate.
