# Shared genetic architecture of cortical morphology and psychiatric disorders

## Key Findings
Cross-trait genetic analyses across 180 cortical regions and 6 psychiatric disorders reveal distinct architectures: internalizing disorders and SCZ/BD share more genetic loci with localized cortical effects, whereas NDDs share fewer loci but with more widespread effects. 17 genomic loci shared across all disorders mostly had opposing directional effects across cortical regions. Only one locus (rs2431112) showed region-specific, unidirectional effects. Directional heterogeneity within pleiotropic loci limits genetic prediction of brain morphology for psychiatric disorders.

## Relevance to Atlas
The finding that NDD-associated loci have more widespread cortical effects (vs. localized for internalizing disorders) is relevant to understanding why NDD mutations produce diffuse phenotypes. The directional heterogeneity finding cautions against simple models linking genetic variants to brain structure to psychiatric phenotype.
