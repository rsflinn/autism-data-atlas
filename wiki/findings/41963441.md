# Base editing restores CDKL5 expression and rescues neuronal deficits in a patient-derived model of CDKL5 deficiency disorder

## Key Findings
Adenine base editing (ABE) corrected a pathogenic CDKL5 variant in patient-derived iPSCs and restored CDKL5 protein expression. The corrected iPSC-derived neurons showed rescue of neuronal deficits associated with CDKL5 deficiency disorder, demonstrating proof-of-concept for base editing as a therapeutic strategy for this NDD.

## Relevance to Atlas
CDKL5 is in our 49-gene autism set and is a kinase that phosphorylates MECP2 in the regulatory network we study. This paper provides a patient-derived neuronal model with characterized phenotypes before and after gene correction -- potentially useful for understanding CDKL5's downstream effects on neuronal function and for comparing with other NDD gene models.

## Genes
CDKL5 (directly studied; in atlas gene set). MECP2 (CDKL5 substrate, likely relevant to mechanism).

## Methods
Patient iPSC derivation, adenine base editing (ABE), iPSC-to-neuron differentiation, neuronal phenotyping.
