# Subunit-selective GABAA receptor modulation via neurosteroid DARTs

## Key Findings
Engineered neurosteroid DART (Drug Acutely Restricted by Tethering) platform enables cell-restricted, subunit-resolved GABAA receptor modulation. Lead compound selectively enhances alpha4/delta-containing receptors while sparing gamma2-containing receptors. Complementary benzodiazepine DART shows opposite selectivity. These tools enable dissection of how distinct GABAA receptor subclasses contribute to circuit function.

## Relevance to Atlas
Directly relevant to the receptor-type separation model (GABA burden tracks with epilepsy). GABRB3, GABRG2, and GABRA1 are in the atlas gene set. Tools that can selectively modulate specific GABAA receptor subtypes are critical for testing whether different receptor compositions mediate different aspects of the epilepsy phenotype in autism-epilepsy comorbidity.

## Genes
GABRB3, GABRG2, GABRA1 (atlas genes; alpha4/delta vs gamma2 subunit selectivity directly relevant)

## Methods
Pharmacogenetic DART engineering, electrophysiology, subunit-selective screening of 17 NAS analogs
