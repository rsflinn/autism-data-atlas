# Proteasome dysfunction underlies HERC2-linked neurodevelopmental disorder with Angelman-like clinical features

## Key Findings
Biallelic HERC2 variants cause NDD resembling Angelman syndrome (developmental delay, ID, autism features, movement abnormalities). Quantitative proteomics identified proteasome as most prominently affected complex. HERC2 recognizes unassembled 19S regulatory particle subunits via chaperone interactions and targets them for degradation. Patient fibroblasts (p.Pro594Leu) show impaired 19S processing and aberrant proteasome activity increase.

## Relevance to Atlas
HERC2 is in the same chromosomal region as UBE3A (15q11-13, Angelman/Prader-Willi). While HERC2 is not in the 49-gene core set, the Angelman-like phenotype and proteasome mechanism connect to UBE3A biology. Provides mechanistic insight into how ubiquitin pathway disruption produces autism-like phenotypes.
