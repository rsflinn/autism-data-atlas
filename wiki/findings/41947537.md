# Convergent genetic pathways linking neuropsychiatric and ocular disorders in children

## Key Findings
Using large-scale GWAS data from PGC and UK Biobank, Pan et al. found significant positive genetic correlations between ASD and myopia/astigmatism, and inverse genetic correlations between ADHD and the same ocular conditions. 124 shared loci were identified between psychiatric diseases and refractive errors. Enrichment analyses pointed to early neurodevelopmental processes as the key shared genetic mechanism.

## Relevance to Atlas
Identifies 124 shared noncoding loci between ASD and refractive error phenotypes, expanding the catalog of pleiotropic ASD-associated regions. The neurodevelopmental enrichment of shared loci is consistent with the atlas model that common noncoding variants converge on developmental gene programs.
