# Effect heterogeneity reveals complex pleiotropic effects of rare coding variants

## Key Findings
Systematic investigation of rare variant pleiotropy across thousands of phenotypes in large-scale biobanks. Reveals that single rare coding variants commonly affect multiple traits with heterogeneous effect sizes and directions, revealing complex pleiotropic architectures that cannot be captured by single-trait association studies.

## Relevance to Atlas
Directly relevant to the atlas's core question: how do different genetic variants converge on overlapping phenotypes with variable severity? This paper provides a quantitative framework for understanding why NDD gene variants produce autism in some individuals, epilepsy in others, and variable comorbidities. The effect heterogeneity framework could help explain dataset-dependent convergence patterns observed in atlas analyses.
