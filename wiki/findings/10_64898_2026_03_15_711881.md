# Modeling cis-regulatory variation in human brain enhancers across a large Parkinson's Disease cohort

## Key Findings
More than 100 noncoding GWAS loci have been linked to Parkinson's disease risk. This study models cis-regulatory variation in human brain enhancers across a large PD cohort, using cell-type-specific single-cell data to decipher the functional impact of GWAS variants on gene regulation in specific brain cell types.

## Relevance to Atlas
Highest-scoring paper today. While focused on PD rather than autism, the methodology is directly transferable to autism GWAS loci. All 5 genome-wide significant ASD loci (Grove 2019) are noncoding, and the field lacks cell-type-resolved models of how these variants affect brain enhancer activity. This paper from the Aerts lab (known for enhancer/regulatory genomics) demonstrates the approach needed. The single-cell resolution and brain tissue focus make this particularly relevant.

## Genes
No autism-specific genes, but the enhancer modeling framework could be applied to any of the 49-gene set's regulatory regions.

## Methods
Single-cell data integration, cis-regulatory variation modeling, brain enhancer identification, GWAS locus functional annotation, cell-type-specific analysis.

## Cross-references
Directly relevant to ChromBPNet common variant analysis (completed, negative with caveats). This approach addresses some of the limitations identified in that analysis (adult brain models, wrong cell types).
