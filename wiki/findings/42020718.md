# Cell-type-specific genetic architecture reveals neuronal and immune contributions to neuropsychiatric disorders.

## Key Findings
Wang et al. integrated single-cell eQTL data from brain and blood with GWAS summary statistics for six neuropsychiatric disorders (schizophrenia, Parkinson's, bipolar, MDD, ADHD, ASD) using summary-data-based Mendelian randomization. They identified 345 cell-type-specific risk genes, including replicated hits (MAPT in astrocytes for SCZ/PD; FLOT1 in excitatory and inhibitory neurons for SCZ/BP/MDD) and novel associations (APTX in microglia for SCZ). Brain-derived risk genes showed significantly higher cell-type specificity than blood-derived genes, and cross-disorder analyses pointed to shared pathways in synaptic function and immune regulation alongside disease-specific patterns.

## Relevance to Atlas
Method is directly applicable to the atlas' regulatory-variant pipeline: sc-eQTL plus SMR is a way to connect GWAS noncoding loci to specific cell types without relying on bulk-tissue eQTLs, which miss prenatal and cell-type-restricted effects (the same limitation documented for BrainVar vs. GTEx in project memory). The paper's ASD cell-type risk gene list should be cross-referenced with the 49-gene autism set and with the subset of NDD genes that have fetal-brain eQTLs (MECP2, EP300 and 10 others per project records).

## Genes
None of the 49 atlas genes are named in the abstract. The ASD-specific cell-type risk gene list would need to be extracted from the supplementary data; not verified from abstract alone.

## Methods
Single-cell eQTL (brain + blood) + GWAS summary statistics + summary-data-based Mendelian randomization (SMR) across neuronal and immune cell types.
