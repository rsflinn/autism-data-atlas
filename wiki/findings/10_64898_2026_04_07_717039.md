# STEAM: Evolutionary transfer learning for mammalian enhancer landscapes

## Key Findings
STEAM (Synteny-aware Transfer learning for Enhancer Activity Modeling) uses enhancer orthologs from 241 mammalian genomes (Zoonomia) to train deep learning models that predict cell type-specific enhancers organism-wide. From 3.9M single-cell ATAC-seq nuclei spanning mouse E10-P0, they resolved 36 cell classes and 140 cell types. The model produces 7,712 genome-wide enhancer prediction tracks (32 cell classes x 241 genomes). Evolution-augmented training increases effective data scale 195-fold and markedly improves cross-species generalization.

## Relevance to Atlas
This is a major methodological advance for noncoding variant interpretation. STEAM's cell type-specific enhancer predictions across developmental lineages could be directly applied to score autism-associated noncoding variants for cell type-specific regulatory effects. The developmental atlas (E10-P0) covers the critical window for neurodevelopmental gene programs. Data and models are publicly available.
