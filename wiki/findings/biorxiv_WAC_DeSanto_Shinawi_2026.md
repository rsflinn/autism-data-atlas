# Complementary vertebrate Wac models exhibit phenotypes relevant to DeSanto-Shinawi Syndrome

## Key Findings
First comprehensive vertebrate models of WAC gene disruption. Mouse and zebrafish Wac knockouts recapitulate DeSanto-Shinawi Syndrome features: craniofacial changes, behavioral abnormalities, GABAergic neuron impacts, and seizure susceptibility. Mouse mutants show sex-different brain volume changes and transcriptional consequences of Wac loss.

## Relevance to Atlas
WAC is not in the 49-gene atlas set but the GABAergic neuron phenotype connects to the atlas's GABAergic pathway genes (GABRB3, GABRG2, GABRA1, SLC6A1). The dual autism+epilepsy phenotype is directly relevant to the atlas's receptor-type separation model, though WAC represents a monogenic upstream cause affecting GABAergic circuits rather than a direct ion channel/receptor disruption.

## Genes
No direct overlap with the 49-gene atlas set. WAC affects GABAergic neurons, connecting indirectly to: GABRB3, GABRG2, GABRA1, GABRB2, SLC6A1.

## Methods
Murine and zebrafish Wac/wac deletion mutants. Behavioral testing, seizure susceptibility assays, brain volume MRI, GABAergic neuron quantification, bulk transcriptomics.
