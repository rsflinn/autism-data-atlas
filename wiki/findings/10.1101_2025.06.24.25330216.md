# Cell-type-resolved genetic regulatory variation shapes inflammatory bowel disease risk

## Key Findings
Maps cis-eQTLs across 2.2 million single cells in IBD to resolve cell-type-specific regulatory effects of noncoding variants. Methodologically relevant to autism noncoding work.

## Relevance to Atlas
Methodological template for cell-type-resolved noncoding variant interpretation -- the core challenge in autism's ~49% common variant heritability that lies in noncoding regions.

## Genes
None from 49-gene set.

## Methods
Single-cell RNA-seq across 2.2M cells; cis-eQTL mapping at cell-type resolution
