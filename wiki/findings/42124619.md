# Disruption of the SYNGAP1 PDZ ligand motif accelerates differentiation of human iPSC-derived GABAergic neurons

## Key Findings

Human iPSC-derived GABAergic neurons with SYNGAP1 haploinsufficiency show accelerated maturation: increased dendritic length, synaptic density and structural maturation. Disruption of the SYNGAP1 PDZ binding motif reproduces these phenotypes. Proteomic, phosphoproteomic and RNA-seq analyses show dysregulated synaptic proteins, RNA processing and transcriptional control. Acceleration begins within hours of neuronal induction.

## Relevance to Atlas

Direct evidence that a SFARI 49-gene-set member acts in GABAergic as well as glutamatergic neurons. Relevant to the receptor-type separation reframing -- SYNGAP1 was historically framed as a glutamatergic gene, and this paper shows clear inhibitory-neuron effects. The isoform-specific (alpha1) and PDZ-motif-specific mechanism is also a useful precedent for thinking about how regulatory effects on splicing or domain-specific function can drive NDD phenotypes.

## Genes (from 49-gene set)

SYNGAP1.

## Methods

Human iPSC differentiation to GABAergic neurons, CRISPR PDZ motif disruption, mass spec proteomics and phosphoproteomics, RNA-seq.
