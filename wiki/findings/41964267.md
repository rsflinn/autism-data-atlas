# Reliability and stability of cerebral palsy classification scales for individuals with STXBP1- and SYNGAP1-related disorders

## Key Findings
GMFCS, MACS/Mini-MACS, and CFCS classification scales show appropriate interrater reliability for STXBP1 (N=83) and SYNGAP1 (N=101) cohorts from a prospective natural history study. STXBP1 cohort showed higher reliability (weighted kappa 0.83-0.93) than SYNGAP1 (0.66-0.81). Test-retest stability was lower for SYNGAP1, suggesting more phenotypic variability.

## Relevance to Atlas
STXBP1 and SYNGAP1 are both in the atlas gene set. This paper provides standardized phenotype severity data for two NDD gene cohorts -- relevant to the severity-disruption question. The differential stability between genes (STXBP1 more stable than SYNGAP1) could inform phenotype-genotype correlations.

## Genes
STXBP1, SYNGAP1 (both directly studied; both in atlas gene set).

## Methods
Prospective natural history study, cross-sectional and longitudinal design, standardized functional classification (GMFCS, MACS, CFCS), interrater reliability analysis.
