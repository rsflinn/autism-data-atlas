# Analysis of 14q12 microdeletions reveals novel regulatory loci for the neurodevelopmental disorder-related gene, FOXG1

## Authors

Ramamurthy, A.; Bandouil, M. D.; Aluru, L.; Joshi, O.; Yoon, E.; Bodkin, N.; Cheng, J. Z.; Biar, C. G.; Calhoun, J. D.; Carvill, G. L.

## Key Finding

Microdeletions at 14q12 that spare the FOXG1 coding sequence but fall within flanking noncoding sequence cause neurodevelopmental disorders, identifying novel cis-regulatory elements (CREs) required for FOXG1 expression. Up to 17% of NDDs can be explained by pathogenic SVs, and this study demonstrates that the noncoding fraction is clinically significant.

## Null Expectation / Comparison

The null expectation would be that microdeletions causing NDD at 14q12 require disruption of FOXG1 protein-coding sequence. This study finds disease-causing noncoding SVs that spare coding exons but disrupt regulatory elements — contradicting the coding-centric null and directly supporting the atlas's core hypothesis about regulatory variant importance.

## Methods

SV mapping in NDD patient cohort; comparative genomics for CRE conservation; reporter assays to validate regulatory activity of identified elements.

## Genes Mentioned

- FOXG1 (no wiki page yet — high-priority candidate for creation)

## Atlas Relevance

- Directly supports the [Noncoding Variants in Autism](./../../concepts/noncoding_variants_in_autism.md) framework
- Provides a concrete example of regulatory SVs causing NDD — the clearest class of evidence for CRE disruption
- FOXG1 is a forkhead transcription factor expressed in progenitor cells and interneurons, relevant to the receptor separation model

## Caveats

NDD broader than ASD; FOXG1 syndrome overlaps but is not exclusively autism. Regulatory element characterization is functional in reporter assays, not in native chromatin context.
