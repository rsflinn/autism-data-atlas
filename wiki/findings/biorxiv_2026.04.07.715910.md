# Cell-specific variant-to-gene mapping identifies conserved neural and glial regulators of sleep

## Key Findings
Cell-specific variant-to-gene (V2G) mapping framework applied to excessive daytime sleepiness GWAS. Identifies conserved neural and glial regulators. Demonstrates methodology for connecting noncoding GWAS variants to specific genes in specific cell types.

## Relevance to Atlas
Methodologically relevant: the V2G mapping approach could be applied to ASD GWAS loci. Sleep disturbances are common in autism. The cell-type-specific framework for interpreting noncoding GWAS hits is directly applicable to the project's interest in noncoding regulatory variants.
