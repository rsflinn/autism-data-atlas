# Brain cell type nuclei enrichment without fixative for nanoCUT&Tag and other omics approaches

## Key Findings
Presents a fixative-free workflow for enriching specific brain cell type nuclei (endothelial cells, mural cells, microglia, astrocytes, neurons, oligodendrocytes) followed by nanoCUT&Tag epigenomic profiling. This enables cell-type-resolved histone modification mapping in human and mouse brain tissue without the artifacts introduced by fixation.

## Relevance to Atlas
Directly applicable as a methods resource for the atlas. Characterizing how autism regulatory variants function requires cell-type-specific epigenomic data from brain tissue. This workflow could be applied to postmortem brain samples from autism cohorts to map histone modifications at noncoding risk loci across neuronal subtypes and glia -- exactly the kind of data needed to interpret GWAS hits in cell-type context.

## Genes
None from the 49-gene autism set are mentioned. Methods paper applicable across all loci.

## Methods
FACS-based nuclei sorting from unfixed brain tissue, nanoCUT&Tag (low-input histone modification profiling), multi-omic compatibility.
