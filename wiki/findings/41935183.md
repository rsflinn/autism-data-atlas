# Assessing molecular gene by treatment interactions using a population of neural progenitors exposed to valproic acid and lithium

## Key Findings
Valproic acid (VPA) and lithium treatment of 83 genotyped human neural progenitor lines revealed that gene regulatory responses (measured via ATAC-seq and RNA-seq) are enriched in genetic risk loci for psychiatric disorders including autism. Over 1,000 loci showed genetic variation modulating drug response, with a subset directly affecting psychiatric risk variant function. A VPA-context TWAS identified folate metabolism genes associated with cognitive ability, connecting to the known protective effect of folate supplementation against VPA teratogenicity.

## Relevance to Atlas
This study directly maps the intersection of environmental (VPA as prenatal autism risk factor) and genetic regulatory variation in a neural cell type. The chromatin accessibility and gene expression data from 83 donors provide a population-level resource for identifying regulatory variants that interact with environmental exposures relevant to autism etiology.

## Genes
No genes from the 49-gene autism set are specifically highlighted in the abstract, though the study reports enrichment at psychiatric risk loci genome-wide. Full-text review may reveal overlap with atlas genes at the >1,000 GxT loci identified.

## Methods
Population-scale neural progenitor culture (n=83 donors), ATAC-seq (chromatin accessibility), RNA-seq (gene expression), response QTL mapping, transcriptome-wide association study (TWAS), enrichment analysis against psychiatric disorder GWAS.
