# Mutation timing, accumulation and selection in the male germline shape inheritance risk for developmental disorders

## Key Findings
WGS of 168 parent-child trios combined with ultra-accurate duplex sequencing of paternal sperm. In 127 fathers, sperm mutation burden and spectra were indistinguishable from population controls. Global dN/dS of 1.56 (positive selection) with 28/32 significantly selected genes overlapping prior findings. 6 fathers carried early mosaic pathogenic variants (0.7-14.8% VAF) accounting for ~11% of aggregated exome pathogenic burden. The remaining burden distributed across low-VAF mutations including positively selected driver variants.

## Relevance to Atlas
Directly relevant to understanding de novo mutation origins in developmental disorders. The finding that most fathers of DD children have normal sperm mutation profiles means the pathogenic de novo mutations in most cases arise from universal mutational processes, not from an abnormal germline state. The ~11% attributable to early mosaicism has recurrence risk implications. Relevant to Teagan's case given her negative exome -- understanding the full mutational landscape matters.
