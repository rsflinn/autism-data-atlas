# Modeling cis-regulatory variation in human brain enhancers across a large Parkinson's Disease cohort

## Key Findings
Models cis-regulatory variation in human brain enhancers across a large Parkinson's disease cohort. Deciphers functional impact of noncoding GWAS loci on gene regulation using cell type-specific approaches. Parkinson's-focused, not autism.

## Relevance to Atlas
Methodology is directly transferable to the atlas: cell-type-specific enhancer modeling of noncoding GWAS signal in brain. The approach could be applied to autism GWAS loci (Grove et al. 2019 and successors) to score cis-regulatory impact. Relevant to the ChromBPNet-style workflow.

## Genes
None in atlas 49-gene set

## Methods
Cell-type-specific brain enhancer modeling across a large PD cohort, combined with GWAS fine-mapping.
