## Abstract

This narrative review synthesizes advances from the past 18&#x200a;months on the etiology of autism spectrum disorder (ASD), integrating findings from genetics, neurobiology, environmental epidemiology, and developmental psychiatry. Given the profound clinical heterogeneity of ASD, improved etiologic clarity is essential for risk stratification, early identification, and targeted intervention.
