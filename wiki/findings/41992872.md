# Examining Genetic Variants Associated with FOXP1 Syndrome through Molecular Dynamics of Its DNA-Binding Domain and Self-Organizing Maps

## Key Findings
Molecular dynamics simulations of six missense variants in FOXP1's DNA-binding domain, combined with molecular docking and self-organizing maps. Variants residing in helix H3 show reduced interactions with DNA, providing a structural framework for interpreting FOXP1 syndrome variants.

## Relevance to Atlas
FOXP1 is one of the atlas's core neurodevelopmental transcription factors. This paper offers structural-level interpretation of FOXP1 missense variants that complements the atlas's focus on regulatory edges from FOXP1 to downstream targets. Useful for variant-level interpretation, though limited to coding (missense) rather than noncoding variation. Co-authored by the De Rubeis/Buxbaum lab (Seaver Autism Center).

## Genes
FOXP1

## Methods
Molecular dynamics simulations, molecular docking, machine learning via self-organizing maps.
