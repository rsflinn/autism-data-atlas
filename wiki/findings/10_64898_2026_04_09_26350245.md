# Genome-Wide Association Analysis of Tic Disorders Reveals 6 Independent Risk Loci

## Key Findings
GWAS of 13,247 tic disorder cases and 536,217 controls identified 6 genome-wide significant loci. Gene prioritization highlighted 20 genes including HCN1 (in our 49-gene set) and PCDH9. Integrative analyses localize genetic risk to cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical circuits and specific cell types: dopamine D1/D2 receptor-positive medium spiny neurons, cortical pyramidal neurons, and oligodendrocyte-lineage cells. Extensive genetic correlations with NDD/psychiatric traits but not neurological disorders.

## Relevance to Atlas
HCN1 is in the atlas 49-gene set as an ion channel gene. Its identification as a tic disorder risk gene adds cross-disorder evidence for ion channel involvement in NDDs. The cell-type specificity findings (MSNs, cortical pyramidal neurons) are relevant to understanding which cell types are most vulnerable to ion channel disruption. The NDD-but-not-neurological genetic correlation pattern is informative for the atlas's phenotype mapping.
