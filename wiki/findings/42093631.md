# Targeting glutamatergic pathways: genetic insights into comorbid neurodevelopmental disorders

DOI: [10.1093/brain/awag152](https://doi.org/10.1093/brain/awag152)

## Key Findings
CNV analysis of 72,626 pediatric participants from CHOP (12,472 ADHD; 7,967 with comorbid NDDs) identified significant CNV enrichment in metabotropic glutamate receptor (GRM) genes and their protein-protein interaction networks specifically in ADHD-with-comorbid-NDD cases (FDR<0.05). 27 GRM-interacting genes were enriched, including DLG2, NRXN1, SHANK3, and SYNGAP1. ADHD-only cases showed notably less enrichment, suggesting a distinct genetic architecture for comorbid presentations centered on glutamatergic signaling.

## Relevance to Atlas
Direct relevance to the receptor-type-separation workstream (PARTIALLY FAILED, April 2026). The Glessner finding is consistent with shared glutamatergic involvement across ASD/ADHD/anxiety -- not class-level separation. CNV-based evidence extends the atlas beyond SNV/PTV burden findings. The fact that NRXN1, SHANK3, and SYNGAP1 emerge in GRM-interacting networks is consistent with the "specific shared genes, not receptor classes" reframing being considered for the preprint revision. Notable caveat: this is ADHD-anchored comorbidity, not autism-anchored, so direction of enrichment in pure-ASD cohorts remains untested here.

## Methods
PennCNV calling on CHOP cohort genotype data; STRING database for PPI annotation; enrichment testing of CNVs overlapping GRM genes and their PPI networks vs. ADHD-only and disease-free controls; FDR correction.
