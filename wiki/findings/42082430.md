# A framework to infer de novo exonic variants when parental genotypes are missing enhances association studies of autism.

## Key Findings
Methods paper from Mount Sinai/Pittsburgh/CMU presenting a Random Draw framework to infer de novo exonic variants when parental genotypes are missing. Increases statistical power for autism gene discovery in cohorts without trio data.

## Relevance to Atlas
Methodological tool relevant to autism cohorts with parent-missing data. Limited to exonic, not noncoding variants.

## Genes
None from 49-gene set.

## Methods
Classifier trained on variants of known inheritance class; Random Draw model integrated into TADA framework; code at github.com/HaeunM/TADA-RD
