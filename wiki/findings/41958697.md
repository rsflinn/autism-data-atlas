# Base editing in the AUTS2 gene and high-throughput NGS genotyping of clones

## Key Findings
Used adenine base editor to introduce synonymous SNVs in AUTS2 in iPSCs as allele-specific markers. High-throughput NGS genotyping of 100+ subclones outperforms Sanger sequencing. Selected clones with heterozygous substitutions, confirmed monoclonal origin, identified allele carrying chromosomal rearrangement.

## Relevance to Atlas
AUTS2 is an ASD candidate gene. The approach of tagging alleles with synonymous variants to track cis-regulatory effects is methodologically relevant for studying regulatory variant effects on gene expression.

## Genes
AUTS2 (not in 49-gene core set but established ASD candidate)

## Methods
Adenine base editing (ABE), iPSC culture, amplicon-based NGS genotyping, phasing with germline variants.
