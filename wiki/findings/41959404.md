# SYNGAP1 Synchronizes Relative Neuronal Maturation Across Cortical Areas to Organize Distributed Functional Networks

## Key Findings
SYNGAP1 haploinsufficiency in mice produces opposing cortical activity patterns: sensory-evoked responses show reduced gain while movement/arousal activity is amplified. Layer 2/3 excitatory populations across cortical regions occupy distinct maturation states during circuit assembly. SYNGAP1 loss reduces this developmental separability through region-dependent, opposite-direction effects on dendritic maturation and inversion of ERK-sensitive intrinsic excitability control.

## Relevance to Atlas
SYNGAP1 is in the 49-gene autism set. This paper provides a mechanism for how single-gene haploinsufficiency produces distributed network-level effects through disrupted relative maturation timing. Relevant to understanding how genetic perturbation produces both hypo- and hyper-functional network states simultaneously.
