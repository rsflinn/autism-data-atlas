# Effects of introgressed Neanderthal alleles on present-day brain morphology

## Key Findings
Analysis of 370 MRI-derived brain traits in ~40,000 UK Biobank participants. Low-frequency Neanderthal-introgressed variants are depleted for detectable brain effects (purifying selection), while common introgressed variants show no depletion. 8 associations fine-mapped to Neanderthal-derived variants, notably near DAAM1 with opposite effects in cuneus vs precuneus mediated by regulatory variants. Neanderthal deserts enriched for functional brain effects. Polygenic Neanderthal effects detectable against schizophrenia and toward major depression.

## Relevance to Atlas
Demonstrates at population scale how noncoding regulatory variants shape brain structure with region-specific effects. The framework -- regulatory variants having opposite effects in different brain regions -- is directly relevant to understanding how autism-associated noncoding variants might produce specific neural phenotypes. The finding that purifying selection acted on low-frequency introgressed brain variants parallels the constraint patterns seen in NDD genes.
