# Identification of a Novel Homozygous SCN1B Splice-Site Variant in a Consanguineous Families With Early-Onset Epilepsy

## Key Findings

Two consanguineous Pakistani families carry a novel homozygous canonical splice-site variant in SCN1B (c.591-2A>G), the sodium channel beta-1 subunit. All four affected individuals presented overlapping early-onset drug-refractory seizures, developmental delay, intellectual disability and autism spectrum disorder. Identified via exome sequencing with Sanger validation across families.

## Relevance to Atlas

Adds another sodium channel beta-subunit case to the autism-epilepsy comorbidity boundary that already includes SCN1A, SCN2A, SCN8A in the atlas. The splice-site mechanism (intron acceptor disruption) is functionally a regulatory-region variant that disrupts pre-mRNA processing rather than coding sequence, making it relevant to the broader noncoding/regulatory framing. Recessive inheritance with full penetrance of ASD+epilepsy in this family contrasts with the autosomal-dominant SCN1B GEFS+ literature and reinforces the dose-sensitivity theme.

## Genes

SCN1B (not in current 49-gene autism set; sister to SCN1A, SCN2A, SCN8A which are). Worth flagging for inclusion in the extended ion-channel set given the autism phenotype here.

## Methods

Exome sequencing on index cases, Sanger sequencing for variant validation and family segregation. Case series of two unrelated families, n=4 affected individuals.
