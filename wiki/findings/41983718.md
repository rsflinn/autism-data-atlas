# Missense mutations on SynGAP C2 domain impair membrane diffusion

## Key Findings
Using extensive molecular dynamics simulations, the study defines how the SynGAP C2 domain binds lipid bilayers in two distinct orientations: a "top" mode enabling faster lateral diffusion and a "side" mode with more stable but less mobile contacts. Pathogenic missense mutations in membrane-facing loops disrupt these dynamics, reducing diffusivity and altering membrane avidity. This reveals a dual binding mechanism underlying SynGAP's membrane versatility.

## Relevance to Atlas
SYNGAP1 is in the 49-gene atlas set and is a major autism/NDD gene. This paper provides mechanistic insight into how specific missense mutations alter protein function at the membrane level. While focused on coding variants (not regulatory), understanding the functional impact of SYNGAP1 mutations informs variant interpretation for the entire gene.
