# Functional characterization of 42 CK2α de novo variants associated with Okur-Chung neurodevelopmental syndrome

## Key Findings
42 CSNK2A1 de novo variants characterized enzymatically. 13/42 had zero kinase activity, 12 had <10% activity. However, 5 variants (R21Q, T127M, E264D, E282K, R333*) showed no reduced activity, normal CK2β affinity, and normal thermostability -- suggesting pathogenic mechanisms beyond enzymatic function for a subset of OCNDS cases.

## Relevance to Atlas
Demonstrates that coding variants in NDD genes can be pathogenic through mechanisms invisible to standard functional assays. Relevant to understanding why some NDD variants of uncertain significance score normal on straightforward functional tests.

## Genes
Other genes: CSNK2A1

## Methods
In vitro kinase assays with canonical CK2 peptide substrate; CK2β binding affinity (Kd); thermostability; site-saturation mutagenesis at K198.
