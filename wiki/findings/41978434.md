# Functional characterization of 42 CK2alpha de novo variants associated with Okur-Chung neurodevelopmental syndrome

## Key Findings
42 de novo CSNK2A1 variants linked to Okur-Chung NDD syndrome (an ASD-associated condition) were functionally characterized for enzymatic activity. 13 had zero detectable activity and 12 showed less than 10% of wild-type. Five variants (R21Q, T127M, E264D, E282K, R333*) showed no reduction in activity, CK2beta affinity, or thermostability, suggesting parameters beyond kinase function drive the neurodevelopmental phenotype.

## Relevance to Atlas
CSNK2A1 is not in the core 49-gene set but Okur-Chung syndrome overlaps phenotypically with autism. The finding that some pathogenic variants retain normal enzymatic activity raises the possibility of regulatory or interaction-level disruption rather than simple loss of function -- relevant to the atlas's focus on how different genetic mechanisms converge on overlapping NDD phenotypes.
