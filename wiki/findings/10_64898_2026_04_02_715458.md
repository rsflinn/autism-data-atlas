# Enhancer hubs govern chromatin topology and Th17 identity

## Key Findings
Only ~25% of open chromatin regions demonstrate regulatory activity in ATAC-STARR-seq. Most encode broadly shared latent potential, with a subset showing cell-type restricted activity. CRISPR epigenome editing screens identified core enhancer elements essential for Th17 polarization. Region Capture Micro-C revealed active regulatory hubs formed through CTCF-independent interactions in hierarchical architecture. A critical Batf enhancer operates these chromatin contacts.

## Relevance to Atlas
Methodologically important: demonstrates that functional enhancer screens + 3D chromatin contact mapping can identify hierarchical regulatory hubs controlling cell identity. The finding that only 25% of accessible chromatin is functionally active is directly relevant to interpreting ATAC-seq peaks at autism-associated loci. The CTCF-independent hub architecture is a model for how distal noncoding variants might propagate effects.
