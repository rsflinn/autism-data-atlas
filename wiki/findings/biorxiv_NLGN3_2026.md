# NLGN3 autism variants have distinct functional impact on synapses and sleep behavior in Drosophila

## Authors

Townsley, R.; Andrews, J.; Srivastav, S.; Jangam, S.; Hannan, S.; Kanca, O.; Yamamoto, S.; Wangler, M. F.

## Key Finding

Multiple ASD-associated NLGN3 variants — including the extensively studied p.R451C — have distinct rather than uniform effects on synaptic function and sleep behavior when modeled in Drosophila. The study moves beyond the canonical R451C mutation to compare variant-specific consequences in vivo.

## Null Expectation / Comparison

If all NLGN3 ASD variants were equivalent in effect (loss-of-function only), one would expect uniform synaptic and behavioral phenotypes. The finding of variant-specific outcomes contradicts a simple null model and suggests allelic heterogeneity — consistent with the atlas's observation that convergent gene targets can have divergent mechanistic entry points.

## Methods

Drosophila NLGN3 (Drosophila homolog) CRISPR engineering; electrophysiology at neuromuscular junction; sleep monitoring.
