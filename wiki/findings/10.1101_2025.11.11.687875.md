# Cell-type-resolved NRXN1 isoforms across human brain tissues and hiPSC cortical organoids

## Key Findings
Long-read isoform characterization of NRXN1 across human brain tissues and hiPSC cortical organoids at cell-type resolution. NRXN1's extensive alternative splicing diversifies protein-protein interactions, shapes synaptic specialization and contributes to NDD risk.

## Relevance to Atlas
NRXN1 is in the atlas 49-gene set. Provides cell-type-resolved isoform reference that directly informs how NRXN1 regulatory and splicing variation could translate to synaptic phenotypes. High-priority for NRXN1 variant interpretation.

## Genes
NRXN1

## Methods
Long-read sequencing and cell-type-resolved isoform quantification in human brain tissue and hiPSC cortical organoids.
