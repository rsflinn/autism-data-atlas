# Unraveling the Genetic and Molecular Architecture of Autism Spectrum Disorder

## Key Findings
Comprehensive review of ASD genetic architecture covering chromosomal abnormalities, CNVs, SNVs (de novo and inherited), polygenic contributions, and epigenetic regulation. Notes that diagnostic yields from array-CGH, WES, and WGS remain limited, reflecting ASD's genetic complexity. ASD-associated genes converge on transcriptional regulation, chromatin remodeling, synaptic function, and neuronal signaling pathways.

## Relevance to Atlas
Provides an updated reference for the state of ASD genomic diagnostics and pathway convergence. Reinforces the atlas's framing that multiple genetic mechanisms converge on shared biological pathways. Useful as a citation for the limited diagnostic yield of current genomic approaches.
