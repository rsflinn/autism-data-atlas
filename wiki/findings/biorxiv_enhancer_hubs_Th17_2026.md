# Enhancer hubs govern chromatin topology and Th17 identity

## Key Findings
This study identifies enhancer hubs -- clusters of noncoding regulatory elements that collectively govern chromatin 3D topology and cell-type identity in Th17 immune cells. The work demonstrates that individual enhancers within hubs have non-redundant roles in maintaining chromatin architecture and gene expression programs during cell differentiation.

## Relevance to Atlas
The direct autism relevance is minimal (Th17 immune cells, not brain). However, the enhancer hub framework and chromatin topology methods are directly transferable to understanding how noncoding autism risk variants in enhancer-dense regions (e.g., near MEF2C, TCF4, or other transcription factor loci) affect brain cell-type-specific gene regulation. The conceptual model of hub-level regulatory disruption could explain how single noncoding variants produce outsized effects on gene expression.

## Genes
None from the 49-gene autism set are mentioned. Study focuses on immune lineage transcription factors.

## Methods
CRISPR-based enhancer perturbation, ATAC-seq, Hi-C (chromatin conformation), RNA-seq, computational enhancer hub identification.
