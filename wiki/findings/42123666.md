# A De Novo Variant as a Candidate Driver in a Neurodevelopmental Disorder: Insights from Trio-Based Whole-Exome Sequencing

## Key Findings

Trio whole-exome sequencing on a female patient with ASD level 1, borderline intellectual functioning, coordination disorder and epilepsy identified a de novo USP24 variant (c.3155G>T; p.Ser1052Ile) classified as likely pathogenic. Two paternally inherited variants of uncertain significance in additional NDD-associated genes were also detected. The authors argue for a cumulative-variant model rather than strict monogenic causation.

## Relevance to Atlas

Adds a single-case data point relevant to the project's core question of how multiple variants combine to produce overlapping NDD phenotypes. USP24 (ubiquitin-specific peptidase 24) emerges as a candidate ASD gene from this case and prior literature; not currently in the 49-gene set but worth tracking. The autism + epilepsy comorbidity profile parallels Teagan's. Coding variant case, not noncoding-focused.
