# Whole-genome 3D architectural screen reveals modulators of brain DNA structure

## Key Findings
Plate-C enables in-plate chromosome conformation capture across thousands of whole-genome architectures in a day; Parasar et al. profiled 2,956 samples / 834 conditions across 5 neuronal and glial types, plus single-cell Easy Dip-C on 6,081 cells and 200,893 single-cell transcriptomes. They show cell-type-specific and dose/time-dependent 3D genome changes driven by HDAC, BET, mTOR, UPR, GSK3/Wnt, Hedgehog, cGAS/STING and neurotransmission perturbations. HDAC inhibition rewires the brain genome in newborn mice within hours, producing a latent structural/transcriptional state orthogonal to normal differentiation.

## Relevance to Atlas
Not autism-specific, but directly relevant to interpreting noncoding regulatory variants in brain cells. 3D genome architecture sets the physical substrate for enhancer-promoter contacts affected by autism-associated regulatory variants. Method and data could be used as a reference for cell-type-specific 3D context when scoring noncoding variants.

## Methods
Plate-C (in-plate chromosome conformation capture), Easy Dip-C (single-cell 3C), single-cell RNA-seq across neuronal and glial cell types; in vivo HDAC inhibition in newborn mice.
