# Disrupted glial-mediated synaptic refinement in Fragile X syndrome

## Key Findings
FMR1 knockout mice at P7 show reduced synapse size and accelerated eye-specific segregation in the retinogeniculate pathway. Multi-omic single-cell transcriptomics identified coordinated alterations in microglia, astrocytes and neurons in synaptic pruning genes, with enhanced astrocyte-to-microglia signaling through EphA and semaphorin pathways. Glial engulfment assays confirmed over-engulfment of synaptic material, identifying impaired glial-driven synaptic refinement as an early mechanistic feature of FXS.

## Relevance to Atlas
Provides glial/neuronal scRNA-seq signatures linking Fragile X syndrome — the most common monogenic cause of autism — to synaptic pruning dysregulation. Directly relevant to cell-type-specific regulation of synaptic genes and the FXS-ASD axis. Useful reference for glial contributions to NDD pathways, distinct from the TF/ion-channel cascade focus of the atlas.
