# Non-canonical roles for Hif-1alpha in interneuron progenitor proliferation and cortical circuit maturation

## Key Findings
Preview of Lu et al.: Hif-1alpha plays a non-canonical (hypoxia-independent) role in cortical interneuron development and interacts with Grin2b. Controlled Hif-1alpha expression is essential for brain development without activating the canonical hypoxia-response pathway.

## Relevance to Atlas
GRIN2B is in the 49-gene autism set. This identifies a new regulatory input to GRIN2B function during interneuron development. From the Pasca lab (Stanford), whose organoid data this project has analyzed extensively.
