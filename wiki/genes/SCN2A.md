# SCN2A

## Overview

SCN2A (Sodium Channel Voltage-Gated Type II Subunit Alpha) is a sodium channel gene at the intersection of autism and epilepsy. It represents a rare example of genetic convergence where both rare damaging variants and multiple perturbation-induced downstream effects implicate the same gene in two distinct neurodevelopmental phenotypes.

## Role and Function

**Ion Channel Function:** SCN2A encodes a neuronal voltage-gated sodium channel critical for action potential initiation and propagation.

**Dual NDD Association:** The gene is implicated in both early infantile epileptic encephalopathy (EIEE) and autism spectrum disorder, often appearing in patients with autism-epilepsy comorbidity.

## Key Findings

### Epilepsy Association

**Damaging Missense Mutations:** SCN2A shows significant enrichment of damaging missense mutations in developmental and epileptic encephalopathy.

- Dataset: Epi25 Consortium
- Effect: p = 4.2e-4 for damaging missense burden
- Evidence tier for this finding: B (statistically real, not independently validated in independent cohort)

### Autism Convergence Target

**Multiple Gene Perturbations Converge on SCN2A:** In the CHOOSE multiplexed perturbation screen, knockouts of multiple distinct autism risk genes produce downstream dysregulation of SCN2A.

- Dataset: Paulsen 2023 CHOOSE
- Finding: Convergent target across multiple autism gene perturbations
- Mechanism: Suggests SCN2A sits at a functional bottleneck in autism-associated regulatory circuits

## Comorbidity Boundary Position

**Clinical Significance:** SCN2A's dual status as both a rare variant disease gene (epilepsy) and a convergent target (autism) positions it at the genetic boundary between autism and epilepsy. This suggests:

1. Shared underlying mechanistic vulnerabilities between the two phenotypes
2. Potential explanation for autism-epilepsy comorbidity
3. Possible therapeutic target that could address both conditions

## Evidence Quality

**Evidence Tier: B** -- Real but not specific. The association with autism via CHOOSE is statistically significant and reproducible within that dataset, but has not been independently validated in a second perturbation dataset (e.g., different cell types, different screening modality, or orthogonal validation experiment).

**Caveat:** While the epilepsy association is well-established clinically, the autism convergence finding requires independent validation.
