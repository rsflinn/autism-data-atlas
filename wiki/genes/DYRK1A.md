# DYRK1A

## Overview

DYRK1A (Dual-Specificity Tyrosine-Phosphorylation-Regulated Kinase 1A) is a kinase located within the Down syndrome critical region whose loss-of-function mutations cause DYRK1A syndrome, a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by intellectual disability, autism spectrum disorder, and microcephaly. Located on chromosome 21q22.13, DYRK1A has dual roles: overexpression of DYRK1A contributes to Down syndrome neurocognitive deficits, while loss-of-function causes distinct autism and developmental delay syndrome.

## Role and Function

**Kinase Activity:** DYRK1A encodes a dual-specificity kinase capable of phosphorylating both tyrosine and serine/threonine residues on protein substrates.

**Developmental Regulation:** Has critical roles in brain growth and development primarily by regulating:
- Cell proliferation and neurogenesis
- Neuronal plasticity and dendritic development
- Synaptic transmission
- Cell survival and apoptosis

**Dosage Sensitivity:** DYRK1A function is exquisitely dosage-sensitive; both loss and gain of function cause neurodevelopmental pathology with distinct phenotypes.

## Key Findings

### DYRK1A Syndrome: Loss-of-Function Autism

**Finding:** Disruptive de novo mutations of DYRK1A (frameshift, nonsense) were identified in individuals with syndromic intellectual disability and autism spectrum disorder, distinct from the gain-of-function effects in Down syndrome.

**Clinical Phenotype:**
- Intellectual disability (variable severity)
- Impaired speech development and language delay
- Autism spectrum disorder with anxious and/or stereotypic behavior problems
- Microcephaly
- Distinctive facial gestalt
- Hypertonia or atypical muscle tone
- Feeding problems
- Seizures (reported in subset of cases)
- Gait disturbances
- Foot anomalies

**Genetic Epidemiology:** DYRK1A syndrome accounts for approximately 0.1-0.5% of individuals with intellectual disability and/or autism, making it a significant but rare cause.

### Enrichment of Truncating Mutations in Autism

**Finding:** Comparison with 8,696 controls identified a significant enrichment of DYRK1A truncating mutations in autism and intellectual disability cases.

**Evidence:** Excess of de novo mutations in ASD/intellectual disability patients confirms that loss of DYRK1A function is pathogenic rather than benign variation.

### Paradoxical Dosage Effects: Down Syndrome vs. DYRK1A Syndrome

**Gain of Function (Down Syndrome):**
- Copy number increase (trisomy 21) increases DYRK1A expression
- Overexpression contributes to Down syndrome neurocognitive deficits
- Lithium (DYRK1A inhibitor) rescues some Down syndrome phenotypes in mouse models

**Loss of Function (DYRK1A Syndrome):**
- Truncating mutations reduce DYRK1A kinase activity
- Causes distinct intellectual disability and autism syndrome
- Opposite phenotypic direction from overexpression but similar cognitive impact

**Interpretation:** DYRK1A function appears to operate in a narrow optimal window; both overexpression (Down syndrome) and loss (DYRK1A syndrome) impair normal cognitive and social development.

## Autism Relevance

DYRK1A syndrome demonstrates that autism can result from loss of a developmental kinase required for normal neuronal development and synaptic function. The combination of autism, intellectual disability, and microcephaly suggests DYRK1A controls fundamental aspects of brain growth and neural circuit development. The occurrence of autism in ~50% of DYRK1A syndrome cases, combined with the genetic enrichment evidence, establishes DYRK1A as an autism-relevant gene.

**Evidence Tier: B** -- Real association confirmed by enrichment in ASD cohorts and multiple independent case reports; specific neurobiological mechanism of autism pathogenesis not yet characterized (appears to involve global developmental dysregulation rather than specific circuit pathology).

## Receptor Separation Relevance

DYRK1A affects both glutamatergic and GABAergic neuron development through its control of cell proliferation, neurogenesis, and synaptic development. Loss of DYRK1A impacts global brain growth (evidenced by microcephaly) before lineage-specific differentiation is complete, suggesting effects on both neuron types at early developmental stages. Classification: **shared** (kinase function affecting both glutamate and GABA development through early proliferation and differentiation control).

## Noncoding Regulatory Context

DYRK1A mutations in autism syndrome are predominantly loss-of-function coding variants (frameshift, nonsense). Copy number increases in Down syndrome reflect the gene duplication in trisomy 21 rather than regulatory variants. Cis-regulatory variants affecting DYRK1A expression in normal ranges have not been systematically characterized in autism cohorts. The narrow optimal dosage range suggests that even modest regulatory variation might affect function.

## Key Datasets

- Autism and intellectual disability case cohorts (discovery sets) -- Initial identification of DYRK1A truncating mutations
- Simons Simplex Collection -- De novo DYRK1A variants
- SPARK -- Autism cases with DYRK1A mutations
- Down Syndrome Research and Resource Centers -- Overexpression phenotypes and model systems
- Mouse transgenic models (Dyrk1a-deficient mice) -- Behavioral and neurobiological characterization
- Zebrafish DYRK1A knockout -- Conservation of social impairment phenotype relevant to autism
