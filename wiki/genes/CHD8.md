# CHD8

## Overview

CHD8 (Chromodomain Helicase DNA-Binding Protein 8) is a high-confidence autism risk gene and chromatin remodeler whose disruption causes a distinct neurodevelopmental syndrome characterized by autism spectrum disorder, macrocephaly, and distinctive facial dysmorphism. Disruptive CHD8 mutations are identified in approximately 0.1-0.3% of autism spectrum disorder cases, making it one of the most common single-gene causes of autism with a recognizable clinical phenotype.

## Role and Function

**Chromatin Remodeling:** CHD8 is an ATP-dependent chromatin remodeling enzyme belonging to the SNF2-like helicase family, capable of repositioning nucleosomes along DNA.

**Gene Regulation:** CHD8 acts as a global transcriptional regulator, with binding sites distributed throughout the genome at regulatory regions controlling developmental genes.

**Widespread Expression:** Expressed in nearly all cell types; particularly important in neural progenitors during neurodevelopment.

**Developmental Process Regulation:** Controls multiple cellular processes including:
- Cell cycle and proliferation
- Neuronal differentiation and migration
- Dendritic and axonal development
- Synaptic maturation and plasticity
- Myelination and oligodendrocyte function

## Key Findings

### CHD8 Mutations Cause Syndromic Autism

**Finding:** Integrated analysis of 15 patients with de novo disruptive CHD8 mutations revealed that autism was the most common diagnosis (13/15, 87%), closely followed by macrocephaly (12/15, 80%).

**Clinical Phenotype:**
- Autism spectrum disorder (87% penetrance)
- Macrocephaly with increased head growth (80% penetrance)
- Orbital region overgrowth beginning in first 2 months postnatally
- Distinctive facial dysmorphism
- Variable intellectual disability
- Developmental delay

**Natural History:** Orbital overgrowth appears within first 2 months of life, indicating early neurodevelopmental origin; increased head growth persists throughout early childhood.

### Genome-Wide Transcriptional Effects

**Finding:** CHD8 haploinsufficiency (single allele loss) causes extensive transcriptional dysregulation across the genome.

**Scale of Effects:**
- 1,756 genes with altered expression (64.9% upregulated)
- 7,324 replicated CHD8 binding sites across the genome
- CHD8 binding sites mark 5,658 genes

**Enrichment in Autism Risk Genes:** CHD8 targets are strongly enriched for other autism and neurodevelopmental disorder risk genes, suggesting CHD8 occupies a topological hub position in developmental gene networks.

### Cell-Type Specific Effects

**Mechanism:** CHD8 shows differential effects across neural lineages:
- Strongest effects on neuronal progenitor differentiation
- Major influence on timing of neurogenesis and migration
- Widespread impact on both excitatory and inhibitory lineage development

**Network Integration:** CHD8 targets converge in autism-associated co-expression networks in human midfetal cortex, suggesting that CHD8 controls genes functioning in multiple interconnected developmental modules.

## Autism Relevance

CHD8 represents a striking example of how global transcriptional disruption causes syndromic autism with recognizable developmental phenotype. The macrocephaly and facial dysmorphism indicate that CHD8's role extends beyond brain development into overall craniofacial and body growth. The 87% autism penetrance and genome-wide effects on other autism risk genes position CHD8 as a major regulator of autism-relevant developmental programs.

**Evidence Tier: B** -- Real and reproducible autism association with syndromic features confirmed across multiple independent cases; genome-wide effects documented but specific mechanistic link between CHD8-regulated genes and autism pathophysiology not yet fully characterized.

## Receptor Separation Relevance

CHD8 affects both glutamatergic and GABAergic neurodevelopment through its control of genes in both lineages. Its broad effects on neural progenitor differentiation and cell cycle control suggest it influences fundamental developmental decisions affecting both neuron types before lineage specification. Classification: **shared** (transcriptional control of multiple developmental pathways in both glutamate and GABA lineages).

## Noncoding Regulatory Context

CHD8 mutations are predominantly de novo loss-of-function variants (frameshift, nonsense) disrupting kinase activity. Cis-regulatory variants affecting CHD8 expression have not been systematically characterized. The widespread chromatin binding and global transcriptional effects suggest that even partial reduction in CHD8 dosage produces measurable developmental consequences.

## Key Datasets

- De novo mutation discovery cohorts (Simons Simplex Collection, SPARK) -- Identification of 15 clinically characterized cases
- Human midfetal cortex transcriptomics (Gandal 2018, others) -- CHD8 target gene co-expression networks
- Mouse Chd8 knockout and heterozygous models -- Recapitulation of macrocephaly and autism-like behaviors
- ENCODE chromatin data -- CHD8 binding sites at regulatory elements
- Paulsen 2023 CHOOSE -- Multiplexed perturbation screen (CHD8 included in the 36 screened genes)
