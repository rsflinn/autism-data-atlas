## Atlas Integration Strategy

### Use SPARK for:
- Large-sample GWAS and common variant analysis
- Phenotype-genotype correlation with deep behavioral measures
- De novo variant discovery (power from size, family structure)
- Combined with SSC for increased de novo statistical power

### Use MSSNG for:
- Structural variant discovery (46% of variants are SVs)
- Regulatory element disruptions via SVs
- De novo SV burden analysis
- Population-specific variants (international diversity)
- Cloud-based scalable analyses

### Use SSC for:
- De novo variant validation and mechanistic studies
- Gene discovery via de novo enrichment (simplex design optimal)
- Sex-stratified genetic architecture
- Phenotypic specificity of genetic findings
- Gold-standard benchmark for validating novel genes

## Recommended Reading Order

### For Atlas Researchers
1. **SSC Overview (2010):** Fischbach & Lord, Neuron - understand simplex design rationale
2. **SPARK Launch (2018):** SPARK Consortium, Neuron - understand largest cohort structure
3. **MSSNG Architecture (2022):** Kaplanis et al., Cell - understand structural variant landscape
4. **Iossifov De Novo Discovery (2014):** Iossifov et al., Nature - seminal de novo findings from SSC

### For Noncoding Variant Focus
1. SSC WGS analysis (2024) - de novo noncoding 4.3% contribution
2. MSSNG structural variants - regulatory SV disruptions
3. SPARK WGS + phenotypes - phenotypic specificity of noncoding variants

## Quick Facts

- **SPARK** = Size and phenotyping champion (largest cohort, deepest behavioral data)
- **MSSNG** = Structural variant champion (only cohort with comprehensive SV data; free cloud access)
- **SSC** = Discovery and validation champion (simplex-optimized for de novo; foundation for gene list)

**Together:** These three cohorts provide complementary strengths for comprehensive autism genetic architecture discovery
